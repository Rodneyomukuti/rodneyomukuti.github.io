About Us

We are a team of wildlife researchers utilizing genomics and genetics to conserve species of conservation concern and the environment at large in Kenya. Our mission is to bridge the gap between conservation genetics/genomics, conservation biology, and conservation ecology, helping conservation managers make informed decisions based on cutting-edge research findings.

Our Projects

  • Corals: Taxonomy, Genetic diversity, Population structure, Connectivity, Resilience, Symbionts, Pathogenic microbes.
  • Turtles: Genetic and environmental drivers of fibropapilomatosis, Nesting patterns, Nesting success, Conservation of nesting beaches.
  • Killifish: Identification (in Kenya) and diversity.
  • Billfish: Evolutionary studies and divergence.
  • Elephants: Population genetics, evolution, and diversity.
  • Monkeys: Taxonomy, Diversity, Evolution.
  • Forensics: Collaborating with KWS to conduct forensic studies on bush meat to aid conservation efforts for endangered wild animals.

RECOURCE

RESEARCH AND CONSERVATION SUPPORT SOCIETY (RECOURCE) is a society with the following objectives:

Promoting the use of scientific evidence by conservation organizations, fostering collaboration among researchers and practitioners, and providing technical support in study design, data analysis, bioinformatics, and scientific writing. We aim to mentor upcoming scientists and conservation practitioners through apprenticeship and offer affiliation for grant applications.
