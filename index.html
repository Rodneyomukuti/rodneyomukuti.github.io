About Us

We are a team of wildlife researchers who utilize genomics and genetics to conserve species of conservation concern and the environment in Kenya. Our mission is to bridge the gap between conservation genetics/genomics, conservation biology, and conservation ecology, helping conservation managers make informed decisions based on cutting-edge research findings.

Research Themes

Population Genetics

Understanding species diversity, connectivity, and evolutionary history.

Corals: Genetic diversity, thermotolerance markers, reef connectivity.
Billfish: Evolutionary genetics for sustainable fisheries.
Elephants: Genetic connectivity, habitat fragmentation effects.
Monkeys: Taxonomy, diversity, and environmental adaptations.
Sea Cucumbers: Genetic diversity for fisheries management.

Microbiome

Investigating microbial communities in marine species.

Coral Symbionts: Microbial interactions for resilience.
Coral-Associated Microbiome: Impact of human activities.

Disease Ecology

Exploring environmental and genetic factors in disease spread.

Sea Turtles: Pollution and genetic factors in fibropapillomatosis.
Coral Pathogens: Molecular detection of coral diseases.

Ecology

Studying species behavior, habitat use, and conservation.

Sea Turtles: Nesting beach monitoring and conservation.

Support

Providing expert conservation support and forensic research.

Forensics: Genetic analysis for wildlife crime investigation.
Killifish: Environmental DNA for species identification.

Our Partners

We collaborate with key organizations dedicated to conservation, research, and environmental protection. Our partnerships strengthen our ability to achieve meaningful conservation outcomes.

Local Ocean Conservation (LOC) is a community-based marine conservation organization in Kenya, focusing on sea turtle conservation, habitat protection, and community education.
Wildlife Research and Training Institute (WRTI) is a Kenyan institution dedicated to wildlife research, training, and capacity building for effective conservation and management of biodiversity.
Olive Ridley Project (ORP) is dedicated to the conservation of sea turtles and their habitats through rescue, rehabilitation, and research efforts.
Maisha Madrugada Foundation (MMF) focuses on marine conservation, sustainable livelihoods, and community engagement to promote ocean health and biodiversity.
REEFolution Trust works on coral reef restoration and conservation, using innovative techniques to rehabilitate degraded reef systems.
Bahari Hai is a Kenyan marine conservation organization that works to protect ocean biodiversity and support sustainable coastal communities.

RECOURSE

Research and Conservation Support Society (RECOURCE) is a society which aims to:

Promoting the use of scientific evidence by conservation organizations, fostering collaboration among researchers and practitioners, and providing technical support in study design, data analysis, bioinformatics, and scientific writing. We aim to mentor upcoming scientists and conservation practitioners through apprenticeship and offer affiliation for grant applications.
